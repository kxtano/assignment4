Exercises for All Levels

Calisthenics exercises can be adapted for all fitness levels. Below, you'll find a variety of exercises ranging from beginner to advanced. Each exercise includes a brief description and tips for proper form to help you get the most out of your workout.

Beginner Exercises

Push-Ups

Start in a plank position with your hands placed slightly wider than shoulder-width apart. Lower your body until your chest nearly touches the floor, then push back up. Make sure your back is straight and you are keeping your core engaged as you do your pushups. There are so many different forms of pushups and I suggest you try different variations that target different parts of your chest.

Bodyweight Squats

Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back as if sitting in a chair. Keep your back straight and chest up. Then rise up back to your starting position. This may be hard for some so you can squat into a chair and work your way up to deeper squats and eventually with a weighted vest.

Intermediate Exercises

Tricep Dips

Find a stable surface like a bench or chair. Place your hands behind you, fingers facing forward. Lower your body by bending your elbows, then push back up. Keep your back close to the chair or bench and keep your knees straight. This is a great workout for your arms.

Pull-Ups

Hang from a pull-up bar or a pole with your palms facing away from you. Pull yourself up until your chin is above the bar, then lower yourself back down. Keep your core tight and avoid swinging, this will keep your back engaged.

Advanced Exercises

Handstand Push-Ups

Kick into a handstand position against a wall. Lower your body by bending your elbows, then push back up. Keep your core engaged and maintain balance throughout the exercise. Pretty difficult workout but with time you can learn it.

Muscle-Ups

Start with a pull-up motion, then transition to a dip motion by pushing your chest over the bar. Lower yourself back down and repeat. This exercise requires significant upper body strength and technique and it takes the most time to learn.
